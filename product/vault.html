1. How to Use This Vault

Every template here has been pulled from cold campaigns that converted at 3%+ reply rate in real B2B contexts. Each one is structured so you can swap the {{variables}} in under 60 seconds and ship.

Three rules before you send anything:

  1. Segment first. A template tuned for a CTO will fall flat on a marketing director. Pick from the right section.
  2. Personalize the first line. The opener is the only line that proves you didn't blast 5,000 people. Spend 30 seconds reading something they wrote, posted, or shipped.
  3. Always send a follow-up. 60% of replies come from follow-ups, not the first email. Use the sequences in Chapter 11.
Rule of thumb: If you're not embarrassed by how short your cold email is, it's probably too long. The shortest version of every template here is the one that wins.

2. The 4 Frameworks Behind Every Template

2.1 — PAS (Problem · Agitate · Solve)

Best for: pain-driven SaaS, services that fix something broken.

Hi {{firstname}},

Most {{role}} teams at {{company-type}} are still {{painful-current-state}}.
That usually means {{visible-symptom-1}} and {{visible-symptom-2}} every quarter.

We built {{product}} to remove that — {{customer}} cut {{metric}} by {{number}}% in {{timeframe}}.

Worth a 15-min call this week?

2.2 — BAB (Before · After · Bridge)

Best for: when the buyer doesn't yet feel the pain — paint the after-state.

Hi {{firstname}},

Right now, {{role}} teams at {{industry}} companies spend {{X hours}} a week on {{tedious-task}}.

Imagine that dropping to {{Y minutes}} — same output, no headcount.

That's what {{product}} does. {{customer}} ran it for {{timeframe}} and freed up {{result}}.

Open to a quick look next Tuesday?

2.3 — AIDA (Attention · Interest · Desire · Action)

Best for: when you have a clear differentiator and a fast hook.

Hi {{firstname}},

{{Bold one-line stat}}.

We help {{ICP}} get to {{outcome}} without {{usual-tradeoff}}. Most teams see {{result}} inside {{30 days}}.

{{Customer A}} and {{Customer B}} are using us this way — happy to share the case study.

Worth a 10-min walkthrough?

2.4 — RDM (Relevance · Differentiator · Micro-ask)

Best for: cold-cold, no warm signal. Wins on micro-ask, not the pitch.

Hi {{firstname}},

Saw {{specific-thing-they-did}} — relevant because we work with {{ICP}} on {{outcome}}.

Most teams come to us when {{trigger}}. We're different from {{competitor-category}} in that we {{differentiator}}.

Not pitching — would a 1-pager be helpful, or worth a 15-min call?
Why these four: Every B2B cold email that gets a reply uses one of them. Every one that doesn't is some unstructured paragraph that buries the ask.

3. Subject Lines That Get Opened (40 Tested)

The "Question" Set (highest open rates, 35–55%)

  • quick question on {{specific-system}}
  • {{firstname}} — is {{painful-thing}} still on your roadmap?
  • worth 10 minutes?
  • {{company}} + {{your-company}}?
  • does this match what you're seeing?
  • {{firstname}}, am I right about this?
  • are you the right person for {{topic}}?
  • quick {{topic}} question
  • open to {{outcome}}?
  • still owning {{function}}?

The "Specific" Set (30–45% open)

  • {{specific-metric}} for {{company}}
  • noticed {{specific-thing}} on {{platform}}
  • idea for {{their-product}} after using it
  • {{their-customer}} → {{relevant-result}}
  • cut {{metric}} 40% — relevant for {{company}}?
  • your post on {{topic}} — quick thought
  • {{competitor}} just shipped {{thing}} — your move?
  • 3 things on the {{their-product}} dashboard
  • after {{their-event}} — one idea
  • {{their-tool}} integration question

The "Curiosity" Set (28–40% open)

  • weird idea
  • this might not be relevant — but
  • two minutes of your day
  • not the usual pitch
  • can I ask a favor?
  • permission to send a 1-pager?
  • am I missing something obvious?
  • thought you'd find this funny
  • caught my eye on {{platform}}
  • this only takes 8 seconds

The "Social Proof" Set (25–35% open)

  • how {{customer}} cut {{metric}} 30%
  • {{competitor-customer}} switched — here's why
  • {{notable-investor}} pointed me your way
  • {{mutual-contact}} suggested I reach out
  • met {{their-colleague}} at {{event}}
  • {{their-customer}} mentioned {{topic}}
  • everyone in {{vertical}} is asking this
  • {{count}} {{ICP}} teams using this now
  • what {{competitor}} won't tell you
  • follow-up from {{event}}
Don't: use ALL CAPS, exclamation marks, "Re:" or "Fwd:" trickery, or "FREE" anywhere. These trigger spam filters and burn domain reputation.

11. Follow-Up Sequences

The 3-Touch (B2B SaaS, mid-market)

  1. Day 0 — initial PAS or BAB email
  2. Day 3 — bump with 1 social proof point: "{{customer}} just hit {{result}} doing this"
  3. Day 7 — Template 47 (permission to close)

The 5-Touch (Enterprise, longer sales cycle)

  1. Day 0 — initial value-driven email (Templates 21–24)
  2. Day 4 — case study or ROI calculator
  3. Day 9 — different angle (champion-builder, Template 23)
  4. Day 16 — Loom or 1-pager attached
  5. Day 25 — break-up email (Template 50)

The 7-Touch (Strategic accounts, multi-stakeholder)

  1. Day 0 — strategic narrative (Template 24)
  2. Day 3 — multi-thread: reach out to second persona
  3. Day 7 — relevant content piece (no ask)
  4. Day 12 — quote-led email (Template 28)
  5. Day 18 — workshop offer (Template 30)
  6. Day 26 — peer or customer success story
  7. Day 35 — break-up

12. Personalization Cheat Sheet

Spend 30-90 seconds on each prospect. Pick ONE of these as your opener — never more than one, never zero.

  • Recent post / article — quote one specific line, react to it
  • Recent product release — name the feature, react to a design choice
  • Funding announcement — congratulate, then connect to a specific implication
  • Job change — reference the move, ask one question about the new role
  • Conference talk — name a slide or moment, build on it
  • Their stack (visible) — name a tool they use, connect to your offer
  • Their customers — name one, draw a parallel
  • Their location — only if you have a real reason (event, meet-up, customer there)
What does NOT count as personalization: "I see you're the {{role}} at {{company}}" / "I noticed {{company}} is in {{vertical}}" / "Hope you're having a great week!" — these signal a blast.

13. Deliverability Checklist

  1. Warm up new domains for 14+ days. Send 5–10 manual emails/day from the domain before scaling. Use Lemwarm, Mailwarm, or Warmup Inbox.
  2. Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. All three. DMARC at minimum p=none for monitoring.
  3. Custom tracking domain. Don't share the platform's default — get a CNAME on your own domain (e.g., links.yourco.com).
  4. Daily volume cap: 30–50 per inbox. Use multiple inboxes if you need more, never one inbox at 200/day.
  5. No images or attachments in cold emails. Plain text, one link max.
  6. Avoid spam trigger words. "FREE", "guaranteed", "act now", "limited time", "$$$".
  7. Verify every email before sending. NeverBounce, ZeroBounce, MillionVerifier. Bounce rate under 3%.
  8. Unsubscribe link on follow-ups. Required for CAN-SPAM. Plain text, end of email.
  9. Reply rate target: 3-7%. Below 3% means your list, copy, or both are off.

14. Legal: CAN-SPAM & GDPR Quick Reference

CAN-SPAM (US)

  • Identify yourself + your physical mailing address (in footer)
  • Don't use deceptive subject lines or "From" addresses
  • Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
  • Include a clear unsubscribe mechanism (visible, working)

GDPR (EU prospects)

  • Cold B2B email is allowed under "legitimate interest" if the recipient is a relevant business contact
  • You must include the same unsubscribe + identification info as CAN-SPAM
  • Keep a record of why each contact is on your list
  • If a recipient asks for their data deleted (Right to Erasure), comply within 30 days
This is not legal advice. Consult counsel for your specific jurisdiction before scaling cold outreach. Rules differ in California (CCPA), Canada (CASL), and the UK (PECR).
